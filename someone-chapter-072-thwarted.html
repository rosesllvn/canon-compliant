A Certain Someone

Ch. 72 · A Certain Someone

Thwarted

某某 by Mu Su Li · fan translation by rosesllvn

Sheng Wang and Jiang Tian had been too distracted looking at WeChat to notice the goings-on at the podium, and hadn't caught the teacher's line about "coming by your dorms tonight to check on you."

At the end of class, the teacher stuck a large chart on the front classroom door. The top row was marked with dates, one cell per day, subdividing the two weeks of camp; the columns were arranged by group—two-person groups, twenty in all.

At first the students wondered what the chart was for and crowded around. Then they watched the speech teacher pull out the past two days' score sheets and, pen in hand, log the marks into the chart.

The day's PK winner got a 1 in that cell, the loser a 0. Sheng Wang and Jiang Tian had won two days running, each with two 1s; Bian Chen and Jiang Tian's unlucky opponent had lost two days running, each with two 0s.

People this age were all more or less competitive, and thin-skinned to boot. To a bunch of praise-accustomed good students, this chart was a public execution—their fighting spirit shot right up.

So that evening the number of students slipping out to play plummeted. Even those who went out came obediently back to the dorms by seven or eight.

The corridor was especially lively that night. The Yizhong boys, to make visiting easier, had all their dorms thrown wide open, the very picture of open doors welcoming guests.

When Sheng Wang and Jiang Tian got back from dinner, the corridor was as packed as a market fair. Several boys ran back and forth among the dorms clutching clothes and towels, and someone asked loudly, "Erzi, how the hell do you not even have body wash?"

"Just ran out, forgot to buy more." A boy in the corridor spat toward the bathroom's little window, "Old tree-bark like you needs body wash? Scrub with soap and be done."

"Screw you."

"You washing or not? If not, come out and let someone else."

"Washing, washing, washing."

Sheng Wang looked thoroughly baffled, half thinking he'd wandered into a public bathhouse. "What are you all doing?"

"Can't you tell? Borrowing a bathroom to shower." Bian Chen was still steeped in the afternoon's PK, an edge in his voice. The guy wore everything on his face—watch him long enough and it almost counted as a kind of frankness.

The boy beside him pointed at the bulletin board by the stairs. "Didn't you read the notice when you came up?"

"Notice?"

Sheng Wang really hadn't noticed.

Jiang Tian stepped back to look. "Water's getting shut off."

"Pipe renovation or something, anyway the water's off tonight," someone explained. "Notice said it starts at 8, but two dorms already had the flow drop too low to shower."

Bian Chen corrected him, "It's three now."

"Oh right, started from that end." The boy pointed to the other side of the corridor. "The girls' floor below's still normal—probably our floor's higher and the pressure's not enough? Anyway it might be out before 8, twenty-odd minutes left. If you want to shower, you'd better hurry."

As he spoke, a howl came from one dorm: "Damn, water's gone. I haven't even rinsed my body wash off!"

The room next door answered at once, "Come over here then? I've still got water, we can squeeze in together."

"Squeeze your grandpa, I'm naked, how am I supposed to get over there?!"

"Cover up and come, idiot!"

"I—the hell with you."

The people in the corridor blanked a second, then cracked up all at once, hollering like demons, "Cover up and come! Cover up and come!"

Not two minutes later, a boy in nothing but underpants, dripping wet and bare-chested, came charging out of one dorm and scrambled hurriedly for another.

Because the body wash was too slippery, he stumbled at the threshold, and a pack of boys rushed over howling with laughter to tug at the waistband of his underpants.

"Damn it, you animals!!!" The boy clawed his waistband and wrenched free, roaring, "You just wait, I'll catch one of you and strip you bare!"

It wasn't that Sheng Wang had never seen dorm life, but he really had never seen anything this unbridled. Eyes seared for a good while, agog, he pushed Jiang Tian to hurry back to their room. Going in, he grumbled, "Here I am being all careful, and they don't have a single scruple between them."

Jiang Tian was typing with his head down, thanking Zhao Xi on WeChat for the help. Hearing this, he didn't catch the meaning and asked offhand, "Careful about what?"

"…"

Sheng Wang shut the door behind him and looked at him in silence.

Jiang Tian turned his head, eyes half-lowered, and after a moment's thought finally understood. His eyelids lifted, his gaze swept over Sheng Wang's eyes, then dropped a little, very lightly.

Sheng Wang felt the door had been warmed by his own back. He was just about to say something when his phone suddenly buzzed in his pocket. He pulled it out—a WeChat message from Sheng Mingyang.

Wellness Encyclopedia: Class over yet? Free to take a call?

Sheng Wang's heart jumped.

He knew of course it was only coincidence, but seeing his dad's message at a time like this carried an irrepressible twinge of guilt.

Jiang Tian hadn't seen the sender clearly; he only seemed to come back to himself and lifted his gaze from the corner of Sheng Wang's mouth. A second later he turned back. "Twenty minutes left. You shower first."

"I'll return a call—you go first," Sheng Wang said.

"A call?" Jiang Tian asked.

Sheng Wang quickly pressed the screen dark, the hand holding the phone dropping. The motion looked careless but carried a hint of concealment. "Old classmate, asking if class is out—probably calling to wish me happy birthday."

Jiang Tian nodded. He tossed the phone by his pillow, took clean clothes from the cabinet, and went into the bathroom first. He tested the water temperature, then came back out to remind Sheng Wang, "Don't take too long, there's not much hot water."

"I know."

Sheng Wang circled the dorm and in the end still went out to the balcony. Elbows on the railing, he stared at Sheng Mingyang's message a good while, until the heart that had just been startled steadied back to normal, and only then typed: Really not free.

Not two seconds after he sent it, the phone started buzzing.

Sheng Wang bit the tip of his tongue and waited a few rings before answering. "Didn't I say I wasn't free?"

There was a smile in Sheng Mingyang's voice. "You think I can't read that little reverse-talk of yours? Class out?"

"Just out."

"Really just out?" Sheng Mingyang said. "It's past seven."

"Then why ask if class was out," Sheng Wang said.

Sheng Mingyang muttered "you brat" over there. "Fine, your dad's used to making small talk, didn't switch gears. I'll humbly admit fault, that work for you?"

"Works," Sheng Wang said.

"Had dinner?" Sheng Mingyang said. "That's not small talk, is it?"

"Just finished," Sheng Wang said. "This time it's for real."

Sheng Mingyang laughed. "Eat what? The food there okay?"

"Cafeteria's so-so. But there are plenty of shops outside the gate, pretty good."

"So you went out to eat with Xiao-Tian today?"

Hearing the two words Xiao-Tian, that twinge of guilt came back. Sheng Wang hunched his shoulders and cracked a knuckle before saying, in a casual tone, "Nah, ate at the cafeteria."

"You didn't go out for your birthday?" Sheng Mingyang was a little surprised. "Oh right—Xiao-Tian doesn't know today's your birthday, does he?"

Jiang Ou's voice came from beside him: "He knows—I told him a while ago, and he said he knew, saw Xiao-Wang's student info over at the discipline office or whichever director's. As his ge, he didn't do anything to mark it? Let me ask Xiao-Wang—"

The moment he heard Jiang Ou was coming to take the phone, Sheng Wang hurriedly added, "We celebrated, did it yesterday. The two of us had a big meal out last night."

For some reason, compared with Sheng Mingyang, Jiang Ou's voice made him guiltier. Luckily, once he'd added that, Jiang Ou's side was reassured and said nothing more.

"Then you should thank Xiao-Tian," Sheng Mingyang said. "Not every ge remembers to celebrate his didi's birthday."

He'd unconsciously slipped back into his business tone. Sheng Wang nodded haphazardly. "Already thanked him."

Sheng Mingyang reminded him to remember Jiang Tian's birthday too, then chatted briefly, and only at Sheng Wang's urging hung up.

He hung over the railing in a daze a while, struck now by a flicker of guilt, now by a flicker of defiance. Not until the balcony door was pushed open behind him did those chaotic, conflicting thoughts find a brief end.

Jiang Tian was drying his hair with a towel; thanks to the wash, the contours of his features looked clean enough to glow under the light. The moment Sheng Wang saw him, all the messy, tangled thoughts got tossed to the back of his mind, and the pleasure that had carried over from early morning slowly poked its head out again.

"Done with the call?" Jiang Tian asked.

"Mm." Sheng Wang came through the balcony door, narrowed one eye to take aim at the upper bunk with the phone in hand, then lobbed it like a free throw—dead center, landing neatly in the thick soft quilt at the foot of the bed. All the buzzing was instantly muffled, as if every outside disturbance had been shut beyond the body.

"I'm going to shower." Sheng Wang took his clothes into the bathroom.

The steam in the space wasn't as thick as before, maybe because of the cold weather—it wasn't even very humid. He'd figured that, washing last, if the water ran short, he'd be the only one out of luck. To his surprise the hot water was more than he'd expected; pick up the pace a little and it was plenty. By the time the flow slowly thinned and cooled, he'd just finished.

Sheng Wang pushed the little window open to vent the steam and walked out drying his hair. Jiang Tian was already sitting at the desk writing tomorrow's speech draft.

With the previous day's lesson learned, they hadn't dared forget the homework again, and had dutifully copied down the speech topic and post-class Q&A at the end of class. Sheng Wang slung the towel over his neck and went to fetch his bag.

He dug out a notebook and pen, pulled out the other chair by the desk, and sat down. But the instant his arm rested on the desk edge, his mind started flashing back to fragments of last night…

His fingers gripping the corner of the desk, head turned slightly. Then, at some point, the grip loosening, knuckles curled with nowhere to settle, and then—catching hold of Jiang Tian's arm.

…

This desk was cursed.

Sheng Wang shot up almost the instant he'd sat down, spun twice with notebook and pen, and, under Jiang Tian's watching gaze, climbed the staircase up to the upper bunk.

"What are you going up there for?" Jiang Tian asked.

Sheng Wang sat down halfway up the wooden staircase and said, in the tone of someone meditating on the Buddha, "Because I feel like it."

Jiang Tian raised an eyebrow but said nothing more. He nodded and bent his head back to his book, white wireless earbuds still in. When he bent his head, the arc of the bone and sinew in his shoulders and back grew pronounced, like a beautiful, sharp bow—shoulders broad, waist narrow, with that quality particular to this age: lean but not frail.

Sheng Wang never wrote whole speeches; he wrote keywords. It was faster, let him adjust on the fly, and avoided the stiff feel of rote memorization.

He jotted scattered phrases in the notebook, and writing along, couldn't help looking up again at his ge's back.

After a moment he pressed his lips, and, as if possessed, stood up again with notebook and pen. He walked back to the desk, silently pulled out that chair, and sat down beside Jiang Tian.

He'd just set his things down when the person beside him suddenly asked, "Why are you back again?"

Sheng Wang was gripping his pen, writing words; he glanced over and drew his gaze back, wrote a few more letters, then said, "Because I feel like it."

The dorm was very quiet, only the rustle of his pen tip sweeping the page. His arm pressed against Jiang Tian's arm, skin touching the other's skin, body heat passing freely between them.

He finished the phrase and at last, in the brimming sense of ambiguity, set his pen down.

He saw Jiang Tian take out one earbud and turn his head, his gaze cast down from half-open eyes.

Their breaths crossed in the gap between their lips, and just as they were about to touch, a "knock-knock-knock" suddenly sounded at the door outside.

Sheng Wang: "…"

What the hell idiot comes at a time like this?!
