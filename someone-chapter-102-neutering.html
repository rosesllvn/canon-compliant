A Certain Someone

Ch. 102 · A Certain Someone

Neutering

某某 by Mu Su Li · fan translation by rosesllvn

He knew, of course, that Sheng Mingyang couldn't think it through in the span of one meal—but he could feel the other's wavering and hesitation, and that was enough. On the way back, he slowly grew happy, even with a careless touch of excitement. But soon he thought of two other people.

"When are Auntie Jiang and Grandpa Ding coming over?" Sheng Wang asked.

Jiang Tian's fingers, replying to a message, paused. "Still a while yet."

Before he'd come back to the country, the sanatorium where Old Man Ding stayed had partnered with a travel agency to arrange a travel-style convalescence for a group of elderly people with similar conditions—keeping their moods relaxed, the travel oriented toward rest and care, not tiring or strenuous, a few days of touring then a stretch of rest. Jiang Ou had gone along—partly to look after the old man, partly to relax and ease herself too.

By the itinerary, they wouldn't reach Beijing until month's end.

Remembering Jiang Ou's once-hysterical state, Sheng Wang still felt a lingering dread. But he also remembered her originally gentle, amiable self, who'd all but spoiled him like a son.

They said travel could relieve stress, and besides, a person's nature was what it was—it couldn't turn from good to bad. So he was anxious and yet held a thread of hope. Even Sheng Mingyang had begun to loosen up; Jiang Ou surely wouldn't fail to soften at all.

Thought of that way, everything was developing in a good direction—it only waited on time.

In good spirits, Sheng Wang drove a detour to Shijingshan.

Jiang Tian wasn't familiar with Beijing's roads, but however unfamiliar he couldn't fail to tell the directions apart—at least he could still read the words on the signs.

He stared at the huge road sign. "You're going back?"

"To get a change of clothes." Sheng Wang had already, without ceremony, claimed Jiang Tian's place as his own turf, deciding on his own to while away the last of the New Year's holiday there. Having said it, he remembered the place's owner sat right beside him, and asked hypocritically, "Can I stay at yours these two days?"

Jiang Tian actually relished this turf-grabbing of his. The lights outside the car flickered in points; the after-effect of dinner's drink was a bit strong. He leaned against the passenger seat back, his voice mild, lazily teasing Sheng Wang. "Give me a reason."

"You're putting on airs now?" Sheng Wang thought. "I want to pet the cat—does that reason work?"

Jiang Tian said mildly, "Denied."

Sheng Wang: "It's literally named Wangzai, and I don't even have the right to pet it?"

Jiang Tian: "Mm, no right."

Without a second thought Sheng Wang changed tack: "Then can I pet you?"

The moment he said it he sensed something off, and the car fell into an eerie silence. Sheng Wang scrambled, "No—I didn't mean to play hooligan in the middle of the street, how about a different verb?"

"Touch? Never mind."

"Play? That's not right either."

The more he explained the worse it got, the more it sounded like a hooligan.

He was about to spit out more words when he heard his ge say flatly beside him, "Shut up."

Sheng Wang finally couldn't hold it and laughed a good while, hand on the wheel, until Jiang Tian gave his head a heavy rub.

Because that string of hooligan talk was genuinely cringe-inducing, and imagining it worse still… in any case the cold, abstinent Dr. Jiang chose silence the whole way, barely humoring him. Only when Sheng Wang got back to the place to pick clothes did he come back online.

Sheng Wang grabbed two sets of home tees and trousers. He said, "I've got those."

Sheng Wang grabbed the change of work shirts for after. He said again, "I've got those."

In short, whatever he grabbed, the answer was "got it," leaving Sheng Wang not knowing whether to laugh or cry, until he finally piled the clothes onto him and asked in earnest, "Ge, tell the truth—do you have some kink about me wearing your clothes?"

Jiang Tian moved his lips, picked up the clothes with a speechless face, and turned and walked off, leaving Sheng Wang eyes full of laughter, rummaging through the storage closet and packing a big bag of stuff.

Jiang Tian put the bulging bag in the back seat and asked, puzzled, "What's this now?"

Sheng Wang fastened his seatbelt and reversed out of the complex. "Cat toys. I'm borrowing a stay two days and taking over its turf—I've got to bring a gift to win its favor, no? A kid raised in a single-parent home has a lot on its mind."

Jiang Tian: "…"

The snow gradually stopped again, a vast white all around, the car cutting through the night, the evening so quiet it bred a thread of laziness.

Sheng Wang stopped at an intersection waiting for the red light, and suddenly heard Jiang Tian say, "Then when do you plan to return it to a two-parent home?"

His voice was low, well-suited to the night. Sheng Wang touched his right earlobe, an itch in his heart. "Doesn't now count?"

"Which two-parent home is one where you pack a bag, stay two days, and run?" Jiang Tian said.

Sheng Wang gave an "oh," and under the red light's countdown turned to look at the passenger seat. "Ge."

"Mm." Jiang Tian answered.

"Are you inviting me to live together?"

"Then will you say yes?" Jiang Tian asked.

The red light jumped to green; Sheng Wang's gaze returned ahead, and he stepped on the gas, teasing, "This is a big deal—I've got to think it over."

In the gap of the red light, he'd imagined, following Jiang Tian's invitation—the two of them living in some corner of the university, keeping a cat together, then slowly persuading family over time.

For an instant he found the life a little familiar, and after a dazed moment realized with a start—this was what they'd pictured of university life that day of Jiang Tian's eighteenth birthday, nestled in the room.

This world sometimes held a kind of predestination. By predestination, they'd still live the days they'd once imagined—only arriving a few years late by accident.

❖

When they got back, the precious cat son of the single-parent home, against all habit, didn't come to greet them, but had both paws clawed on the windowsill peering outward—no telling what life-philosophy it was pondering.

Jiang Tian made a round and found the cat's food bowl was empty.

He'd just opened the cat-food box when that life-pondering lookout came flying over, circling his trouser leg, rubbing its head and face on him, nose tipped up to nuzzle and play sweet.

Sheng Wang's big bag of cat toys had sat silent at home a long while; finally seizing a chance to play, he dumped it all out at once, unwrapping and testing each one.

This person had a sofa and wouldn't sit on it, sitting cross-legged on the carpet instead, tumbling about with the cat.

Jiang Tian observed a while beside them and found a certain someone, swearing he meant to "win the cat's favor," was doing nothing but asking for a beating. The kitten stood on its hind legs, reaching a paw for the cat teaser. He insisted on yanking a cat foot in an ambush, then watched his son lose its footing and flop to the floor.

The cat, riled, turned to run, and he insisted on pinching one of its hind feet—letting it kick three paws in the air, but it couldn't get away. Pushed to it, the kitten stretched a paw eager to swat him, only for him to high-five it.

After a few rounds, the cat didn't dare come over at all, lying aggrieved on the windowsill.

However Sheng Wang shook and waved the teaser, it wouldn't humor him, and he couldn't help turning to ask Jiang Tian, "Why does it keep looking out the window? When I wanted a cat before I researched it—they said if a cat keeps wanting to run out, it might be in heat."

Jiang Tian: "…"

A bellyful of retorts, not knowing which to pick, he finally said sourly, "It's not in heat, it's been neutered."

Sheng Wang gave an "oh" and went back to fiddling with his teaser.

A few seconds later, he abruptly caught it and whipped around. "What did you say? You had it neutered?"

Jiang Tian was momentarily lost. "Mm, what about it?"

"You called it Wangzai, and then you had it fixed???" Sheng Wang's face was full of disbelief.

His expression was genuinely vivid; Jiang Tian blanked a moment and couldn't help it—holding a little cloth mouse, he laughed.

"You're still laughing?" Sheng Wang threw down the teaser and pounced, hauling his ge off the sofa, tickling him and saying, "Downright ill-intentioned—why didn't you call it Little Jiang instead? Don't you run—"

Jiang Tian dodged with a low laugh. "How old are you, still pulling this?"

Sheng Wang said righteously, "I'm eighteen!"

Cursing and laughing, he reached into Jiang Tian's trousers, meaning to say how about you taste that flavor too? But in the tussle, the two, tangled together, struck up a fire.

Sheng Wang braced on the carpet, color rising bit by bit.

He pulled Jiang Tian down and kissed him, then kissed along his chin to his Adam's apple. Just as he meant to make a little mischief, he felt a hand reach in.

He abruptly bent a leg and gripped Jiang Tian's wrist—wanting to stop him yet not in the least firm, more like helping in disguise. A moment later, eyes narrowed, forehead pressed to Jiang Tian's neck, a haze misting his eyes.

Jiang Tian's Adam's apple was red too, his gaze falling beneath thin lids; at the other's unbearable in-between, he suddenly stilled his hand.

Sheng Wang, unable to bear it, turned his head and bit him, and called "Ge" in a hoarse voice.

Jiang Tian shut his eyes and opened them again, watching the other's habitually clear pupils abruptly mist over, then lowered his head and sealed the sound back into his throat.

…

By the time the two were done, the carpet was a wreck, and the cat had long since slipped off somewhere.

Sheng Wang reached for a glass of water, drank a couple of mouthfuls, and handed it to Jiang Tian. Still not satisfied, he kissed the other's chin and asked, as if for fun, "Ge, do you know there's another way too?"

He was a grown-up after all, and reckoned Jiang Tian knew—it was just a hooligan line off the tongue, a bit of fun and done. But his ge, between sips, swept a glance from the corner of his eye and said, "Don't know."

"…"

Sheng Wang thought, are you serious? He pressed, puzzled, "Even if you haven't seen it—you haven't heard of it either?"

Jiang Tian withdrew his gaze and tipped his head back for another sip. Then, elbow braced on a bent knee, his lean fingers turning the rim of the glass round and round, he asked, "No. Care to demonstrate?"

Sheng Wang: "I…"

At this he finally confirmed it: a certain someone's skill at playing deaf and dumb to mess with him was downright masterful.

❖

They say appetite for food and sex is human nature; some things are fine left unmentioned, but once mentioned, you can't help thinking on them a bit.

At seventeen, Sheng Wang had felt he simply couldn't be touched—kissing Jiang Tian a while got a rise out of him, and roughhousing, groping, and bumping into being shut in the bathroom was a frequent thing. That couldn't even be called youthful vigor and inner fire—it was a sun living in his body.

Later, after Jiang Tian left, he turned ascetic. Every day was stuffed with too many things, so busy that even sleep was something snatched, so naturally there was no time to think of such idle matters.

Now, everything had changed again.

Sheng Wang sat cross-legged on the floor, and in a gap between scrolling his phone, glanced for the third time at Jiang Tian's hand petting the cat. Seeing the lean white fingers flicker in and out of the fur, he'd always think of the scene not long ago of those fingers sinking into fabric, and the outline of the knuckles tightening and loosening beneath it…

He stared a few minutes, then climbed up with a composed face, dug an iced water from the fridge and glugged a couple of mouthfuls, then grabbed a change of clothes and went into the bathroom for the second time. He felt he'd returned to his inner-fire-blazing seventeen, with that sun living in his body—no, the sun rising—again.
