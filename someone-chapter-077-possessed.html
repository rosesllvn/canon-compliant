A Certain Someone

Ch. 77 · A Certain Someone

Possessed

某某 by Mu Su Li · fan translation by rosesllvn

When Sheng Wang walked into the classroom, the Class B math teacher happened to be up at the podium handing back the lunch-break practice papers. He'd deliberately gone in by the back door, but it did no good—the whole class turned to look at him under cover of passing papers, their gazes thick with envy.

A student's envy was utterly simple: if you had a legitimate reason not to come to class, you were the happiest person in the world. A class officer called away to a meeting for one period could earn a "so lucky," never mind someone like Sheng Wang who'd been gone half a month.

"Stop looking, there's no answer key growing on my face." Sheng Wang felt he'd accidentally walked onto a red carpet; sitting down, he cupped his hands and begged off.

The whole class burst out laughing, and the math teacher leaned on the podium teasing him, "Sheng Wang's in a good mood—looks like camp life went all right."

Someone quick-tongued chimed in, "Not going to class is more nourishing."

The class went off into another round of laughter before finally settling down to the problems.

Sheng Wang knew he was done the moment he started—half a month of continuous camp had left a slight aftereffect: his first instinct on seeing a math problem wasn't to draw a diagram, set up an equation, or calculate, but to translate the problem into English.

Normally he had more than enough time on a half-hour practice paper; today, thanks to the cursed aftereffect, he was actually a little tight. He'd just finished the last line when the teacher called time.

"Seems a bit rusty, hm, Sheng Wang?" The math teacher jerked his chin at him across a few desks. "Quite a bit slower than before."

This time Sheng Wang had nothing to argue and obediently took the scolding.

"Since the English is wrapped up, you'll need to put more thought into the other classes from now on—do a couple of sequence problems for fun when you've got nothing on, for instance."

The whole class let out a unanimous cry of "ugh."

The math teacher glared at them, then reminded them, half-joking, half-serious, "You haven't touched any of this in half a month, but the other students didn't relax one bit. The weekly exam's in a few days—better get on it."

At the mention of an exam, the class fell into wails. Sheng Wang's mind ran opposite to everyone else's: he hoped the exam would hurry up. Not out of arrogance, but because he hadn't formally sat any exam since midterms, and he was anxious to test back into the top 45 and set Class A's He Jin, Sister Jing, and the rest at ease.

The moment the teacher left, the wailers instantly came bouncing back to life. He Shi was first off the mark, sneaking over hunched from the front row, and Shi Yu volunteered most of his stool to her.

"Hey Sheng Wang, was camp fun?" As someone who'd also sat the English prelim, He Shi's envy was far thicker than the others'.

"It was okay. The camp was so-so, the people were the fun part," Sheng Wang said.

He Shi was tickled. "Everyone who gets to go is a big shot—were there fun people?"

"Sure, Jiang Tian." Sheng Wang was sending a WeChat message, eyes down, and just said it offhand.

He Shi: "…"

Sheng Wang glanced at her and Shi Yu, fingers flying across the keys.

Anniversary Sale: Jiang Tian: fair of face, clear of voice, his very name a strange art that quiets a crying child by night.

Someone: …

Someone: What set you off

Anniversary Sale: A little couple set me off

Someone: ?

Anniversary Sale: Shi Yu and He Shi have an empty chair right there and insist on squeezing onto one

Anniversary Sale: You know He Shi?

Anniversary Sale: Never mind, you might not remember—anyway she's Shi Yu's girlfriend.

Anniversary Sale: Forty-some seats in the whole class, and they choose to flaunt it right in front of me

Anniversary Sale: Big deal having a partner

Anniversary Sale: Well I'm a big deal too

In the Class A room, most people had put away their pens and paper to nap, and only a few were up to something furtive. Gao Tianyang was throwing notes back and forth with the person in front, but his aim was off and he kept hitting Little Pepper's desk, then pressing his palms together to beg her, by every god and ancestor, to pass it to the front desk.

Little Pepper helped while rolling her eyes, and on the fifth round trip Gao Tianyang turned to glance at the back desk, catching that one instant of Jiang Tian's expression.

"Tian-ge," Gao Tianyang said in a low voice, "you were just smiling, weren't you? I didn't see wrong, right?"

Jiang Tian lifted his eyes from under the desk. "You saw wrong."

"I don't care, I saw it," Gao Tianyang said. "Your didi said all I have to do at times like this is argue with you irrationally and stir up trouble."

Jiang Tian didn't follow. "My didi?"

"Sheng-ge."

"…"

Jiang Tian's gaze swept to the phone under the desk—a certain didi was still saying it was a big deal to have a partner.

Before he could reply, Gao Tianyang asked again, "Well, since you were just smiling, your mood should be okay, right?"

"Don't drag my mood into it." Jiang Tian pressed the screen dark and lifted his head, fully clued in. "What've you set me up for this time?"

"It's not my fault this time! I even spoke up for you, but with you not here the deterrence wasn't as strong."

Gao Tianyang turned and waved at the two in the front row, signaling them to hurry the hell over. The next second, Song Sirui, the arts committee rep, and class monitor Li Yu all came rolling over together.

The moment he saw Li Yu and the arts committee rep, Jiang Tian understood something. To Gao Tianyang and the other close friends he could still say "get lost"; to two girls it wasn't so convenient, especially since the class monitor cried easily.

Gao Tianyang poked Li Yu, Li Yu poked the arts committee rep, and the arts committee rep braced herself: "It's like this, Jiang Tian. The campus culture-and-arts festival is on again at the end of the month. Since Senior Grade 3 doesn't take part, this is our last one. He Jin's view is not to take up too much study time, but not to phone it in either."

"Originally a whole-class chorus would've been the fairest, easiest thing—nobody can dodge it, anyway. Pick an easy song, rehearse a few times and you're basically set. But—"

Gao Tianyang pointed downstairs. "Got snatched by the brutes in Class B and Class 7."

The arts committee rep explained, "Those two classes' arts reps came out of the meeting and, without even consulting their classmates, filled out the form and turned it in on the spot. A grade gets at most two choruses, so I democratized things a little, and the slots were all snatched up."

Jiang Tian knit his brow. "So?"

"So we can only do small acts. You know the school rule—if a single act has two people or fewer, that class has to put on two acts. Otherwise the whole grade would be solos." Gao Tianyang pointed at himself and Song Sirui. "The current plan is me and Old Song doing crosstalk, that's one group, and you in a duet with Carp—"

Jiang Tian: "???"

"Pah—no, said it wrong." Gao Tianyang corrected himself. "You play guitar, Carp sings."

Jiang Tian was baffled. "Who said I can play guitar?"

Carp said tremulously, "I can't really sing all that well either."

Jiang Tian: "…"

Gao Tianyang explained, "Me and Old Song were born to do crosstalk in the first place. Carp, as class monitor, sacrifices the small self and steps up first. But Carp gets nervous easily—a solo would probably end in tears. So…"

Jiang Tian: "I can't play."

"It's fine, you know how an arts festival works, don't you? Handsome is enough—who actually goes to appreciate the guitar?" Gao Tianyang said. "Tian-ge, I'm not buttering you up, but with a face like yours, you could hold a broom on stage and people would clap."

"…" Jiang Tian looked at him, hard to describe. "So you cooked up this lousy idea?"

Gao Tianyang shrank his neck. "How would I dare court death like that."

Carp said, "Actually it was Teacher He."

Jiang Tian's face went blank, and after a moment he said, "I'll go find her after class."

"He Jin seems to be out observing classes this afternoon."

"Then I'll find her after school," Jiang Tian said.

But when school actually let out, he didn't manage to corner He Jin—instead he got cornered himself. The person cornering him was surnamed Sheng, given name Wang: the nemesis he'd brought upon himself.

"Heard you're putting on an act too?" Sheng Wang sat with his legs apart on the low window ledge at the stairwell corner, looking up as Jiang Tian came down.

Jiang Tian turned to stare at Gao Tianyang. "You told him?"

Gao Tianyang, having just come down one step, hurriedly shrank back into the classroom. "I didn't bring it up on my own, Sheng-ge just happened to ask."

Jiang Tian went on down the stairs toward Sheng Wang. "I'm not doing it."

"Don't be like that." Sheng Wang stood up with his bag. "I was just counting myself lucky."

"Lucky about what?"

"Our class chorus—while I was away from school, they set my position dead center in the front row," Sheng Wang said. "A pack of animals held off telling me until the afternoon, ruined my mood for the last period. Just heard from Lao Gao that you're performing too, and felt a little consoled."

"Not doing it," Jiang Tian said. "Can't play at all."

"Guitar?" Sheng Wang egged him on. "Crash-learning one easy song is pretty quick—a top student afraid of that?"

The top student was impervious. "Not learning."

"Give it a try."

"No."

"You've got the heart to leave me to embarrass myself alone?"

Jiang Tian tugged his bag strap and said, with utter shamelessness, "Mm."

Sheng Wang narrowed his eyes, then hooked an arm around his neck and pressed him into a hunch, head bowed. "Say that again?"

Jiang Tian's Adam's apple caught on his arm and moved a few times—only Sheng Wang knew he was laughing low.

Gao Tianyang and Song Sirui finally poked their heads out of the classroom, cheering Sheng Wang on across the stairwell while staying ready to duck back in at any moment.

Sheng Wang shot them a glance and turned, clamping Jiang Tian to face away. Behind them was the endless stream of students rushing toward the cafeteria. He lowered his voice and said to Jiang Tian, "Let me tell you a secret—your underground-affair boyfriend happens to know guitar, and he's dying to teach you. One-on-one, satisfaction guaranteed, free of charge. So are you learning or not?"

…

So that evening, Gao Tianyang gave Carp and the entertainment officer some good news: "Tian-ge agreed."

"Seriously?" The two girls could hardly believe it.

"Sheng-ge said so at dinner, and Tian-ge didn't argue," Gao Tianyang said. "Guaranteed genuine."

"Why? How'd he suddenly agree?"

"How would I know," Gao Tianyang said. "Are my Tian-ge's thoughts something mere mortals can guess? Right, Tian-ge?"

And then he turned and asked, "So why?"

Jiang Tian didn't even look up. "Possessed."

Gao Tianyang: "…"

For all the talk of holding a campus arts festival, the only ones really invested were the first-years; the second-years mostly practiced very little, taking up a few evening study periods at most.

Class A had it even easier. He Jin was generous, especially with Jiang Tian—he handed over a long-term leave note outright, saying that if Jiang Tian wanted to practice during evening study he could go.

But Jiang Tian didn't take up many evening sessions, because Class B was watched closely and Sheng Wang couldn't get out. Even with a leave note, it was the whole class going to the music room together to practice the chorus.

Thursday night, after the last proper evening study period let out, Jiang Tian grabbed his bag and was about to head to the lecture hall to find Sheng Wang, when he got a WeChat from him on the way down the stairs.

Anniversary Sale: Come to the arts building

Someone: You went to practice the chorus?

Anniversary Sale: Mm

Anniversary Sale: We've already broken up, I got the key to the music equipment room from the teacher, and got the boarders' evening study leave

Fuzhong's arts building was on the north side, close to the sports field and far from the buildings where the three grades had class. All of Fuzhong's music and art classes were held here, and the art students usually practiced over here too—some of the diligent ones left every day right at the 11 o'clock curfew.

When Jiang Tian ran downstairs, he saw Sheng Wang waiting at the door.

At this hour most of the arts building was dark, only a scattering of classrooms still lit. Sheng Wang glanced upward and said, "There's hardly anyone left—good thing we were quick, or we wouldn't have had long to practice even by eleven."

Jiang Tian came up three steps at a stride and walked shoulder to shoulder with him into the building. After a few seconds, he spoke. "You really called me here to practice?"

Sheng Wang rubbed the bridge of his nose and turned to look behind him. At the arts building entrance, and at the corners of the corridor, there were 360-degree dome cameras.

The school was so big that a few more cameras was perfectly normal. They were meant for anti-theft security, but in the eyes of a guilty-conscienced student, they were the all-seeing eyes of Big Mouth Xu from the discipline office.

Sheng Wang had never felt it before; now he felt it deeply.

The ring-shaped corridor wasn't narrow, but his shoulder and arm kept brushing Jiang Tian's. With no proper pretext, all he could do was use the little bumps and jostles to get a bit closer to the person he liked.

There were still two art students in the first-floor studio, and the music equipment room was right next door. They walked out of the light and into the dark; Sheng Wang lowered his eyes and unlocked the door with the key.

The equipment room wasn't actually small, but it was divided into several narrow lanes by row after row of iron racks. The racks were custom-made, holding different instruments sorted by category—aside from the pianos and other things inconvenient to move, most of them were in here.

"So much dust." The smell of dust in the equipment room was a little heavy, carrying a stale odor, but he didn't lift a hand to fan it away.

The real art students brought their own instruments; only those needing something on short notice came here to fetch it, so even with the arts festival approaching, it was still deserted.

Sheng Wang reached out to turn on the light, but his finger touched the switch without pressing it. He swept the area with the glow of his phone screen and asked, "Would there be a camera in here?"

Jiang Tian looked around too. "Don't see one."

Sheng Wang nodded.

He met Jiang Tian's eyes and asked, "Then does this count as behind closed doors?"

Jiang Tian glanced at his phone screen, then looked back at him. "Not quite."

Sheng Wang flicked his thumb and the screen went dark. The iron racks and canvas bags sank into blackness; clutter was piled by the window, crisscrossed enough to nearly block the whole pane, the corridor light falling through the gaps very faint.

They could see the shadows outside, but from outside, inside was pitch black.

Sheng Wang glanced toward the window and said, "I think we can forcibly count it."

He'd barely finished when voices came from the staircase directly opposite the equipment room—because of the quiet of the night, they heard it clearly.

"You brought your own recorder?"

"Got it from the equipment room."

"Then don't you have to return it?"

"…"

Without a word, Sheng Wang reached up and slapped the light on, and the two of them walked over to the guitar rack one in front of the other.

They'd just picked up a cloth bag when the equipment room door opened. Three girls walked in. "Eh?"

"Jiang Tian?!" One girl called out reflexively, then hurriedly covered her mouth, clearly not having expected to open the door to a handsome guy—and more than one at that.

"You're here for equipment too?" Only after asking did they remember to introduce themselves. "We're from Class 10."

Jiang Tian didn't look to be in a great mood, but he was always cold, so everyone was long used to it. It was Sheng Wang who also seemed a little unhappy—though his voice carried a smile, his expression was very flat. "Came to borrow a guitar. Heading off."

They registered in the booklet hanging at the door and carried the black bag upstairs. This time Sheng Wang had lost his interest in choosing a classroom; he just found an empty one at random and went in.

Seen from above, the arts building's design looked like a musical note, the classrooms and connecting corridor curving into a not-quite-circle, with a green planted garden in the middle. A great stretch of bamboo grew there, dappling the view outside the inner windows—blocking it quite thoroughly, in fact.

Young Master Sheng, his hooliganism interrupted, was unusually put out; he set down the guitar and started sulking. By the time Jiang Tian shut the door and turned, a certain someone was already sitting on the windowsill, having tugged out the hood of his inner sweatshirt and pulled it on.

The light wasn't on yet. He sat in the shadow—cool enough, sure, just with a bit of a temper.

Jiang Tian looked at him, then suddenly walked a circuit along the other side of the classroom, drawing all the curtains that faced the corridor, then locking the front and back doors.

He walked to the window, shrugged the bag off his shoulder and tossed it aside, pulled down Sheng Wang's hood, and bent to kiss him.

In the weather of late December, the night chill ran deep. Sheng Wang had one foot braced on the windowsill, his back against the icy glass, gripping the back of Jiang Tian's neck.

They'd been brothers, roommates, and classmates for several days; it was rare to have only the two of them. The kiss was a little messy, and only after a long while did it slowly turn tender.

❖

Fuzhong's cafeteria in the morning wasn't as crowded as at noon. Plenty of students gave up hot food for a little more sleep, making do with some biscuits or bread.

Whether Sheng Wang and the others went to the cafeteria always depended on their mood; that morning he and Jiang Tian were in a good one, so they sat down early in the cafeteria—only to run into Gao Tianyang and the rest.

That pack of slackers from Class A showing up for breakfast was a genuinely rare sight. Sheng Wang called out a greeting, and the seats around them filled up in an instant.

"Heard you went to practice guitar with Tian-ge last night?" Gao Tianyang shoveled in a mouthful of noodles and looked up. "How'd the practice go?"

Jiang Tian sat across from him, glanced over at the words, and said, "Not great."

"Why?" Gao Tianyang asked.

Sheng Wang and Jiang Tian both had long legs; under the table they were practically interlaced. He knocked Jiang Tian's knee, but kept his eyes on Gao Tianyang as he said, "Guitar's no good."

"Oh, oh, oh, true." Gao Tianyang had no idea about the little maneuvers under the table and even found their words quite reasonable. "It's from the equipment room after all—borrowed back and forth, definitely won't be especially good. So what now?"

"There's one at home." Sheng Wang looked at Jiang Tian. "Go grab it after the weekly exam?"
